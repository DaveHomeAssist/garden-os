# Garden OS: 12-Chapter Campaign Design

> Each chapter teaches one major mechanic, raises the score target, advances the story, and deepens the relationship to Mom's raised bed.
> The campaign escalates in both system mastery and emotional meaning.

---

## Year 1: Learning the Bed

---

### Chapter 1: "First Light"
**Year 1 — Spring**

1. **Title:** First Light
2. **Year and season:** Year 1, Spring
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Basic placement. The player learns to select crops, place them on the 8x4 grid, and erase mistakes. The grid is introduced as a physical object with rows and columns, not an abstraction.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** None. This is ground zero.
5. **Score target:** 4.0 average. Fill at least 12 cells.
6. **Narrative beat:** The bed has been empty for two seasons. The player finds it under a tarp. Garden GURL arrives, surveys the state of things, and delivers the first line of the game. Onion Man is already here. He has been waiting.
7. **Character spotlight:** Garden GURL introduces herself. She sets the standard. Her opening line is also the game's thesis statement: "This bed has good bones. Let us see if you do."
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "You placed eleven crops and created twelve problems. That is almost impressive." Onion Man: "It is a start. Starts are allowed to be rough. The Phillies have proven this many times."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Functional first draft. No hard violations. You are not hopeless." Onion Man: "Okay. Okay. That looks like something. That looks like the beginning of something."
10. **Unlock reward:** Crop Notes panel unlocks. Each crop the player uses gets a one-line annotation visible in future seasons.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the first time the player sees the bed. The empty grid, the tarp coming off, the first crop placed. Every garden game has a first planting moment, but this one is weighted by the fact that someone else built this bed and stopped using it. The player is not starting fresh. They are continuing.

---

### Chapter 2: "Vertical Law"
**Year 1 — Summer**

1. **Title:** Vertical Law
2. **Year and season:** Year 1, Summer
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Trellis and support. Climbing crops must be placed in trellis rows (rows 0-1). Placing them in access rows triggers a hard violation. Non-climbers in trellis rows trigger an advisory for wasted infrastructure.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Basic placement, cell count.
5. **Score target:** 5.5 average. All support crops must be in trellis rows.
6. **Narrative beat:** The trellis has been here since Mom installed it. It is wire and cedar, simple and solid. Garden GURL explains it like building code. Vegeman appears for the first time in a tutorial cameo. He puts a tomato in the front row. It collapses. He does not understand why.
7. **Character spotlight:** Vegeman's introduction. He is confident, wrong, and likable. The player learns what not to do by watching him do it.
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "You put climbers on the ground. The trellis is right there. It has been right there for years." Vegeman: "In my defense, I thought the tomato would figure it out."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Trellis compliance confirmed across both rows. The wire is doing what it was built to do." Onion Man: "Mom put that trellis up herself. Seeing it used right does something to me."
10. **Unlock reward:** Trellis Zone overlay. The board now visually differentiates trellis rows from access rows with persistent color coding.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the first time the game says no. The first hard violation. The first time the player realizes the bed has rules that are not suggestions. And Vegeman's introduction makes the lesson land through comedy instead of lecture.

---

### Chapter 3: "Good Neighbors"
**Year 1 — Fall**

1. **Title:** Good Neighbors
2. **Year and season:** Year 1, Fall
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Companion planting and adjacency. Crops placed next to compatible neighbors receive a score buff. Crops placed next to hostile neighbors receive a penalty. The player can now see adjacency relationships before placing.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Basic placement, trellis compliance.
5. **Score target:** 6.0 average. At least 3 companion pairings.
6. **Narrative beat:** Onion Man takes the lead. He talks about the neighborhood. How Mom knew her neighbors. How the tomato seedling she started with came from the guy two doors down. Good neighbors matter in gardens and on blocks.
7. **Character spotlight:** Onion Man. This is his chapter. He connects the adjacency mechanic to something human without being heavy about it.
8. **Failure flavor:** Onion Man: "You put enemies next to each other and expected peace. That is not how gardens work. That is not how anything works." Garden GURL: "Three conflict penalties. The adjacency report is hostile."
9. **Success flavor:** Onion Man: "Good neighbors make good gardens. Also good neighborhoods. I have been saying this." Garden GURL: "Companion bonus confirmed on three pairings. The bed is cooperating."
10. **Unlock reward:** Adjacency Preview. Hovering a crop over a cell now shows green (companion) or red (conflict) indicators on neighboring cells before placement.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** The mechanic is intuitive but the framing elevates it. The player is not just learning a system. They are hearing Onion Man talk about community in a way that makes the grid feel like a street.

---

### Chapter 4: "Dormant"
**Year 1 — Winter**

1. **Title:** Dormant
2. **Year and season:** Year 1, Winter
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Season review and score history. The player does not plant. They see their Year 1 scores in sequence, their best and worst cells, their most-used crops, and their first-year trajectory. The history system becomes visible as a persistent record.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** None actively. This is a reflective pause.
5. **Score target:** None. This chapter is not graded.
6. **Narrative beat:** The bed is empty again. Covered. Onion Man talks about the offseason. How waiting is part of the work. Garden GURL reviews the numbers without sentiment. The Phillies are in hot stove mode. Onion Man has opinions.
7. **Character spotlight:** Both narrators, in contrast. GURL gives the data. Onion Man gives the feeling. The player sees both perspectives on the same year.
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "Year one data logged. There is room for improvement. Significant room." Onion Man: "The offseason is for planning. The Phillies know this. Sometimes."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Year one average: above threshold on all three seasons. Clean record to build on." Onion Man: "We did that. Three seasons. Real food potential. I need a minute."
10. **Unlock reward:** Season Journal. A persistent log that records each season's best row, worst cell, harvest highlights, and one narrator quote. Visible from the main menu going forward.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the only chapter where nothing grows. The bed is dormant. The player is asked to look backward instead of forward. It is a quiet beat in a game that has been building momentum, and it teaches the player that rest and review are part of the cycle.

## Year 2: Understanding the System

---

### Chapter 5: "Quick Harvest"
**Year 2 — Spring**

1. **Title:** Quick Harvest
2. **Year and season:** Year 2, Spring
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Crop factions and diversity bonus. The roster is organized by faction (Climbers, Fast Cycles, Brassicas, Roots, Greens, Herbs, Fruiting, Companions). Using crops from 4 or more factions in a single season grants a diversity bonus. Fast-cycle crops (radish, lettuce, arugula) are highlighted as gap-fillers.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Trellis compliance, companion adjacency, cell count.
5. **Score target:** 6.5 average. Use crops from at least 4 factions.
6. **Narrative beat:** Year 2 opens with confidence. The player knows the bed. Garden GURL introduces factions as a strategic lens: "You have been planting by instinct. Now plant by faction." The fast crops teach that not every cell needs a long-season anchor.
7. **Character spotlight:** Garden GURL as strategist. She shifts from judge to advisor. The tone is: I see potential, now let us get serious.
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "Three factions in thirty-two cells. That is not a garden. That is a monoculture with excuses." Onion Man: "Variety matters. In food, in life, in a rotation. We have discussed this."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Five factions represented. Diversity bonus confirmed. The bed is thinking in systems now." Onion Man: "That is a salad, a side dish, and a garnish in one bed. Real kitchen energy."
10. **Unlock reward:** Faction Filter. The crop panel can now be filtered by faction, making roster scanning faster during planning.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the chapter where the game stops feeling like a series of rules and starts feeling like a strategy system. Factions give the player a vocabulary for thinking about composition, not just individual placements.

---

### Chapter 6: "Heat Check"
**Year 2 — Summer**

1. **Title:** Heat Check
2. **Year and season:** Year 2, Summer
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Seasonal events. The August Heatwave strikes. Cool-season crops (lettuce, spinach, peas, radish) take a scoring penalty. Heat-tolerant crops hold or gain. The player must plan around a forecastable environmental pressure, not just static rules.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Trellis, adjacency, faction diversity, seasonal crop selection.
5. **Score target:** 6.0 average despite the heatwave penalty.
6. **Narrative beat:** The event is announced at the start of the chapter: "Summer forecast: sustained heat. Plan accordingly." Garden GURL delivers it like a weather advisory with legal implications. Onion Man sweats. Literally and emotionally.
7. **Character spotlight:** Garden Critters make their first narrative appearance. The heatwave is not a character, but the Critters comment on the stress it creates: "Hot week. Weak stems. Found something." The yard is alive and unsympathetic.
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "The engine predicted this. The weather confirmed it. Your lettuce did not survive the confirmation." Critters: "You left stress in plain sight. We just followed up."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Heat event absorbed. Warm-season crops carried the bed. Adequate preparation." Onion Man: "We survived August. That is not nothing. The Phillies cannot always say the same."
10. **Unlock reward:** Event Forecast panel. Future seasons now show a weather preview during the planning phase, giving the player one piece of advance intelligence before committing.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the first time the game pushes back. The player has been building skill in a controlled environment. Now the environment has an opinion. The heatwave forces adaptation and introduces the idea that planning is not enough; you also have to anticipate.

---

### Chapter 7: "Underground"
**Year 2 — Fall**

1. **Title:** Underground
2. **Year and season:** Year 2, Fall
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Row-based objectives. The chapter requires the front row (row 3) to be filled entirely with Roots faction crops. This introduces the idea of zone-specific composition targets, not just whole-bed average scoring.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Trellis, adjacency, factions, seasonal event awareness.
5. **Score target:** 5.5 average. Front row must be 100% Roots faction.
6. **Narrative beat:** This is Onion Man's chapter. He is emotional. He is always emotional, but more so now. The roots go underground. They are not flashy. They do not climb or fruit visibly. They do the work below the surface. He relates to this.
7. **Character spotlight:** Onion Man at his most sincere. He talks about roots as the part of the garden nobody sees but everybody depends on. He does not make it a metaphor. He just says it plainly and it lands.
8. **Failure flavor:** Onion Man: "The front row needed roots and it got chaos. I am not angry. I am disappointed. Those are different and both are happening." Garden GURL: "Row 4 objective failed. Three of eight cells are non-compliant."
9. **Success flavor:** Onion Man: "All roots. Front row. Underground union is pleased. I may have cried a little. It is biologically ambiguous." Garden GURL: "Row objective complete. Roots faction occupancy confirmed. Clean compliance."
10. **Unlock reward:** Row Objective system. Future chapters and challenges can now set per-row composition targets, adding a layer of spatial constraint to planning.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It narrows the player's focus to one row and one faction, which paradoxically makes the rest of the bed harder to manage. The constraint creates real tradeoffs. And Onion Man's quiet pride in the roots carries genuine weight because he has earned the player's trust over six chapters.

---

### Chapter 8: "The Seedling"
**Year 2 — Winter**

1. **Title:** The Seedling
2. **Year and season:** Year 2, Winter
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Narrative memory. The game surfaces a specific detail: the neighbor who gave Mom her first tomato seedling. This is not a mechanic in the scoring-engine sense. It is a narrative system that connects the bed's history to specific crops. Going forward, tomatoes carry a faint annotation: "Original seedling line."
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Season review, score history, crop notes.
5. **Score target:** None. Winter chapter. Review and reflection.
6. **Narrative beat:** Onion Man tells the story. It is short. The neighbor's name is not important. What matters is that the bed started with a gift, and the player is now two years into continuing what that gift became. Garden GURL notes that the cedar is holding up. The bed is aging well.
7. **Character spotlight:** Both narrators again, but the balance shifts. Onion Man carries the story. GURL carries the material reality. Together they give the player two reasons to keep going: the meaning and the structure.
8. **Failure flavor:** Not applicable. No scoring.
9. **Success flavor:** Onion Man: "Two years of scores. Two years of seasons. The bed is not just surviving. It is becoming something." Garden GURL: "Year two data archived. Cedar condition: stable. Trellis wire: intact. The infrastructure is sound."
10. **Unlock reward:** Crop Lineage tag. Certain crops now carry a "lineage" marker connecting them to the bed's history. Tomatoes get the first one. This is cosmetic but emotionally persistent.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the moment the bed stops being the player's project and starts being a continuation. The seedling story is small and specific. It does not demand a reaction. It just sits there, and the player realizes the bed has been someone else's story longer than it has been theirs.

## Year 3: The Real Garden

---

### Chapter 9: "The Board"
**Year 3 — Spring**

1. **Title:** The Board
2. **Year and season:** Year 3, Spring
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Challenge objectives. The player can now accept optional constraints before planting: Trellis Compliance Audit, Diplomatic Immunity Run, Root Faction Loyalty Test, and others. Challenges add bonus score on completion but tighten the solution space.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** All prior mechanics. This is the first chapter that assumes full system literacy.
5. **Score target:** 7.0 average with one challenge active.
6. **Narrative beat:** Garden GURL introduces challenges as "recommended by me, which makes them mandatory." The tone shifts from learning to proving. Year 3 is not about new rules. It is about mastery under constraint.
7. **Character spotlight:** Garden GURL at her most demanding. She is not teaching anymore. She is testing. The respect in her voice is conditional on performance.
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "Challenge failed. The constraint was clear. The execution was not. Retry with intent, not hope." Onion Man: "Close. That was close. I could feel it almost working."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Challenge passed. Constraint honored. Score above threshold. The bed operated under pressure and held." Onion Man: "That was not luck. That was planning with teeth."
10. **Unlock reward:** Challenge Ladder. A persistent progression of challenge tiers with medals. Completing challenges across seasons builds a mastery record.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the chapter where the game respects the player enough to demand more. The training wheels are gone. The score target is real. The challenge constraint makes the familiar bed feel new again.

---

### Chapter 10: "Full House"
**Year 3 — Summer**

1. **Title:** Full House
2. **Year and season:** Year 3, Summer
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Full-bed scoring. The player must fill all 32 cells and score above threshold. This is the first time the game requires complete occupation, which means every placement decision ripples across the entire board. No empty buffer cells to absorb mistakes.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** Every mechanic. Trellis, adjacency, factions, events, row objectives, challenges.
5. **Score target:** 7.0 average with all 32 cells filled.
6. **Narrative beat:** This is the skill check. Nine chapters of escalation converge. The bed is full for the first time. The player must manage every interaction, every tradeoff, every row. Vegeman reappears for the first time since Chapter 2.
7. **Character spotlight:** Vegeman's return. He has been watching. He says: "I told you to plant everything everywhere. You just did it correctly. I am confused." It is the funniest line in the game, and it works because the player earned the full bed through nine chapters of discipline.
8. **Failure flavor:** Garden GURL: "Thirty-two cells, eleven violations. Filling the bed is not the same as planning the bed." Vegeman: "Okay, that one might have been too full. Is that a thing? I did not think that was a thing."
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "All thirty-two cells scored. Average above threshold. No hard violations. This bed is operating at capacity." Vegeman: "You did the thing I always wanted to do, but you did it smart. Honestly, I am a little jealous."
10. **Unlock reward:** Bed Density badge. A persistent marker that the player achieved full-bed mastery. Unlocks a "dense planting" challenge variant for future replays.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the most mechanically demanding chapter. The full bed forces the player to use everything they have learned. And Vegeman's return recontextualizes the entire tutorial: the player started by watching him fail at maximum planting. Now they succeed at it. The arc is complete.

---

### Chapter 11: "Sauce"
**Year 3 — Fall**

1. **Title:** Sauce
2. **Year and season:** Year 3, Fall
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Harvest inventory. For the first time, the post-simulation screen does not lead with the score. It leads with a list of what the bed produced. Tomatoes. Basil. Peppers. Carrots. Onions. The game checks whether the harvest contains the ingredients for Mom's sauce recipe.
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** All mechanics, but the score is secondary to the harvest output.
5. **Score target:** Any passing score. The real objective is the ingredient list.
6. **Narrative beat:** This is the emotional peak of the campaign. The harvest is real. Onion Man reads the list slowly. Garden GURL calculates the yield. Neither of them says anything dramatic. The bed produced enough for sauce. The player can see it. The recipe is not shown as text on screen. It is implied by what grew.
7. **Character spotlight:** Onion Man. This is his moment. He has been building toward this for eleven chapters. His line: "She would have made sauce with this. I am not crying. The onion is crying."
8. **Failure flavor:** Onion Man: "We are close. We are missing basil. Or maybe peppers. The recipe needs all of them and the bed gave us most of them. Most is not enough for sauce. Most is enough for next year." Garden GURL: "Harvest inventory incomplete for target recipe. Recommend adjusting fall composition."
9. **Success flavor:** Onion Man: "Tomatoes. Basil. Peppers. Onions. Carrots. That is the list. That is her list. She would have made sauce with this. I am not crying. The onion is crying." Garden GURL: "Harvest inventory matches target recipe. Yield sufficient. Season objective complete."
10. **Unlock reward:** Mom's Recipe card. A persistent item in the Season Journal. It does not contain the recipe. It contains the harvest list and the date. That is enough.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is the reason the game exists. Eleven chapters of learning mechanics, raising scores, and managing a grid converge in a list of vegetables that add up to a recipe. The player did not know they were working toward this. The game never told them the recipe was the goal. But when the harvest list appears and Onion Man reads it, the player understands what the bed was always for. This is the moment the game earns the word "care."

---

### Chapter 12: "Endures"
**Year 3 — Winter**

1. **Title:** Endures
2. **Year and season:** Year 3, Winter
3. **New mechanic introduced:** Legacy view. The player sees their complete 12-chapter history: every score, every best row, every worst cell, every challenge, every harvest, every narrator quote saved in the Season Journal. The bed is winterized. The camera pulls back (metaphorically, in a single-file HTML game, this means the UI shifts to show the full record instead of the grid).
4. **Returning mechanics tested:** None actively. This is the final review.
5. **Score target:** None. This chapter is not graded.
6. **Narrative beat:** The bed is covered. The season is over. The year is over. Three years are over. Garden GURL delivers a final assessment. It is the longest thing she has ever said, and it is still only three sentences. Onion Man delivers a final feeling. It is the shortest thing he has ever said, and it hits the hardest.
7. **Character spotlight:** Both. Together. For the last time in the campaign.
8. **Failure flavor:** Not applicable.
9. **Success flavor:** Garden GURL: "Twelve seasons logged. The bed is structurally sound. The operator is competent. That is the highest compliment I have." Onion Man: "The bed is ready for next year. So are you."
10. **Unlock reward:** The game loops. Free play opens with all mechanics, all challenges, all events, all factions, and the Season Journal carrying forward. The campaign is over. The bed continues.
11. **Why this chapter is memorable:** It is quiet. It is short. Nothing happens except looking back. The player sees twelve seasons of decisions compressed into a record, and the record tells a story the player did not consciously write. The bed endures. The player can keep playing, but the story is told. The last thing on screen is the bed, covered, waiting for spring. And the player knows what to do when spring comes, because they learned.
