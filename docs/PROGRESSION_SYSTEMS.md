# Garden OS: Progression Systems

Progression in Garden OS is about earned competence, household usefulness, and accumulated relationship to one specific bed. It is not loot, XP grind, or power inflation.

## 1) Campaign Progression

The 12-chapter campaign advances on four linked tracks:

| Track | What grows | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| System mastery | mechanics depth, score targets, pressure combinations | keeps strategic depth alive |
| Bed relationship | familiarity with this exact 8x4 layout | emotional continuity |
| Household meaning | pantry outcomes, recipe milestones | makes outcomes concrete |
| Legacy ownership | keepsakes, notes, repeatable routines | turns inheritance into stewardship |

Each chapter should answer:
- What new pressure can the player read now?
- What can they control intentionally now?
- What does the bed trust them with now?

## 2) Between-Season Progression

Between-season flow should feel like taking stock indoors, not menu chores.

| System | What happens | Progression value |
|---|---|---|
| Season review | best row, weakest row, best save, worst mistake | reinforces learning |
| Pantry carryover | ingredients persist into recipe goals | gives utility to harvest |
| Bed condition | trellis wear, soil fatigue, lane clutter | continuity and consequence |
| Journal update | short tactical note + memory annotation | emotional accumulation |
| Next-brief choice | 1 of 2-3 chapter modifiers | agency without bloat |

## 3) Character and Dialogue Unlocks

Dialogue unlocks are behavior-driven, not chapter-count driven.

| Character | Unlock pattern | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Garden GURL | clean compliance, lower preventable errors | shifts from strict policing to measured respect |
| Onion Man | useful harvests, recipe progress, successful salvage | deeper but restrained emotional lines |
| Vegeman | risky builds, greedy failures, high-variance play | more aggressive temptation lines and comic admissions |
| Garden Critters | repeated weak points vs disciplined prevention | targeted environmental callouts |

## 4) Recipe Unlocks

Recipes are milestone proofs of useful care, not crafting loops.

Proposed ladder:
- Herb bowl / salad night
- Grilled veg side
- Tomato sandwich day
- Weeknight pasta
- Pepper-onion skillet
- Late-summer sauce prep
- Mom's sauce recipe (Chapter 11 apex)

Recipe completion should provide:
- one narrative artifact,
- one practical chapter-level benefit,
- one journal memory entry.

## 5) Garden Journal Progression

The journal is the long-form retention spine.

| Layer | Early | Mid | Late |
|---|---|---|---|
| Player notes | basic observations | tactical shorthand | confident pattern language |
| Crop notes | generic rules | bed-specific behavior | advanced pairings and warnings |
| Family notes | sparse mentions | recurring habits | integrated seasonal routine |
| Season summaries | confusion + correction | intention + tradeoffs | fluency + stewardship |

## 6) Cosmetic Unlocks

Cosmetics must signal care history, not vanity noise.

Good categories:
- Bed material states (repaired corners, clean edging)
- Trellis/twine/stake style variants
- Journal visual treatment (tabs, labels, clipping)
- Pantry/kitchen labeling styles
- Seasonal ambiance overlays (subtle only)

Avoid:
- flashy rarity skins
- novelty clutter
- systems disconnected from bed stewardship

## 7) Memory Objects and Keepsakes

Keepsakes should be milestone artifacts with practical context.

Examples:
- Old plant-tag bundle (early campaign)
- Twine/tool tin (trellis mastery)
- Bent cage segment (salvage chapter)
- Porch colander / jar labels (pantry milestone)
- Handwritten sauce card (Chapter 11)

Presentation: one compact shelf/pocket view between seasons.

## 8) Mastery Ranks

Ranks describe judgment quality, not character level.

Proposed rank arc:
1. Bedhand
2. Rowkeeper
3. Trellis Tech
4. Season Planner
5. Yield Steward
6. Legacy Keeper

Inputs:
- score consistency,
- objective completion,
- salvage quality,
- recipe milestones,
- challenge medals.

## 9) Achievement Categories

- System discipline (trellis/access/compliance)
- Recovery mastery (salvage under pressure)
- Goal harvests (purpose-driven yields)
- Behavior archetypes (greedy, disciplined, companion-heavy)
- Character trust milestones
- Campaign memory beats

Achievements should read like reputation in a neighborhood garden, not platform filler.

## 10) Legacy Mode and Remix Ideas

### Legacy Mode (post-campaign)
- Keeps journal, keepsakes, recipe state, mastery rank
- Harder brief combinations
- Fewer intervention opportunities
- Higher consistency demands
- No power inflation
- No alternate "true ending"

### Remix Seeds
- Curated chapter variants (heat-heavy, no-trellis, access-tax, critter pressure)
- Same board, altered pressure model
- Supports comparison and community challenge runs

### Featured Brief Runs
- Optional seeded runs with fixed conditions
- No FOMO rewards
- Designed for analysis and sharing

## 11) Meaningful Progression Without Bloat

Design rules:
1. One unlock should serve multiple purposes (system + narrative + identity).
2. Prefer fewer deeper systems over many shallow systems.
3. Keep progression visible on the bed and in household outcomes.
4. Reward restraint and judgment, not repetition volume.

Non-goals:
- no currency loops,
- no loot economy,
- no skill-tree inflation,
- no live-service compulsion mechanics.

Core thesis:
> Garden OS progression is the slow accumulation of practical judgment, trust, and continuity in one specific backyard system.
