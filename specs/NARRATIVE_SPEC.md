# Garden OS: Linear Narrative Specification

> Chapters 1-12. Every line deterministic in trigger. No line begs the player to feel something. Feeling arrives through precision.

---

## Chapter 1: "First Light"
**Year 1 — Spring | Score Target: 4.0 | Mechanic: Basic Placement**

### OPENING
The bed is under a blue tarp. Rain has pooled in the center fold. The player pulls the tarp back and sees 32 empty cells of cedar and soil, built by someone who does not use them anymore. No music. Ambient neighborhood noise: a screen door, a distant radio, traffic on Broad Street.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
GURL: "This bed has good bones. Let us see if you do."

GURL: "Select a crop. Place it in a cell. Erase if you reconsider. When you commit the season, placements lock. That is not a suggestion."

Onion Man: "Just start somewhere. The first one does not have to be perfect. It just has to go in the ground."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player places their 8th crop.

Onion Man: "Eight in. That is a quarter of the bed. Starting to look like somebody lives here again."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Functional first draft. No hard violations. You are not hopeless."

Onion Man: "Okay. Okay. That looks like something. That looks like the beginning of something."

**Unlock:** Crop Notes panel. Each crop the player uses gets a one-line annotation visible in future seasons.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "You placed eleven crops and created twelve problems. That is almost impressive."

Onion Man: "It is a start. Starts are allowed to be rough. The Phillies have proven this many times."

**What changes:** Score thresholds remain the same for Chapter 2, but GURL's opening line in the next chapter references the poor showing.

### TRANSITION
The season resolves. The bed produces something small. The screen fades to a warmer palette. Summer light fills the yard. GURL's voice: "Summer forecast loading. The bed is not finished with you."

## Chapter 2: "Vertical Law"
**Year 1 — Summer | Score Target: 5.5 | Mechanic: Trellis and Support**

### OPENING
The trellis is visible for the first time. Wire and cedar, running along the back two rows. It has been here since Mom installed it. The wire has a slight green patina. Vegeman is standing in front of it holding a tomato seedling sideways.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
Vegeman: "Watch this. I am going to put this tomato right here in the front."

The tomato collapses. It falls over in the simulation preview.

Vegeman: "In my defense, I thought the tomato would figure it out."

GURL: "Climbing crops go in trellis rows. Rows zero and one. The wire is structural, not decorative. Placing climbers elsewhere is a hard violation."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player places a non-climbing crop in a trellis row.

GURL: "That crop does not climb. It is occupying infrastructure it cannot use. That is wasted support capacity."

Onion Man: "Mom put that wire up for a reason. Not everything belongs everywhere."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Trellis compliance confirmed across both rows. The wire is doing what it was built to do."

Onion Man: "Mom put that trellis up herself. Seeing it used right does something to me."

**Unlock:** Trellis Zone overlay. The board visually differentiates trellis rows from access rows with persistent color coding.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "You put climbers on the ground. The trellis is right there. It has been right there for years."

Vegeman: "In my defense, I thought the tomato would figure it out."

**What changes:** Chapter 3 opens with GURL noting the trellis violations are on record. The player carries the notation into their Season Journal.

### TRANSITION
Vegeman exits. He does not announce it. He is just gone. Summer heat lingers in the UI. The palette shifts to amber and rust. GURL: "Fall crops rotate differently. The bed remembers what you planted."

## Chapter 3: "Good Neighbors"
**Year 1 — Fall | Score Target: 6.0 | Mechanic: Companion Planting and Adjacency**

### OPENING
The light is lower. The yard has that Philadelphia October quality where everything looks sharper because the haze is gone. Onion Man is leaning on the fence. He has been thinking about neighbors.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
Onion Man: "You know what makes a block work? People who get along next to each other. Same thing in the bed. Some crops help their neighbors grow. Some start fights."

GURL: "Adjacency scoring is now active. Compatible neighbors receive a score buff. Hostile adjacency incurs a penalty. Hover before you commit. The preview will show you who tolerates whom."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player creates their first companion pairing (e.g., tomato next to basil).

Onion Man: "That is not just smart. That is dinner with a plan. Tomato and basil next to each other like they know what is coming."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Companion bonus confirmed on three pairings. The bed is cooperating."

Onion Man: "Good neighbors make good gardens. Also good neighborhoods. I have been saying this."

**Unlock:** Adjacency Preview. Hovering a crop over a cell shows green (companion) or red (conflict) indicators on neighboring cells before placement.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "Three conflict penalties. The adjacency report is hostile."

Onion Man: "You put enemies next to each other and expected peace. That is not how gardens work. That is not how anything works."

**What changes:** Conflict penalties carry a minor soil-fatigue marker into the next year. GURL notes it during the winter review.

### TRANSITION
The harvest is small but real. Onion Man names what grew. The bed goes quiet. Leaves drop in the yard. The UI cools. GURL: "Winter incoming. The bed rests. You should review."

## Chapter 4: "Dormant"
**Year 1 — Winter | No Score Target | Mechanic: Season Review and Score History**

### OPENING
The bed is covered again. Different tarp, same posture. The yard is gray and still. No planting interface. The UI shows three season cards from the year: spring, summer, fall. Each one holds a score, a best row, a worst cell. The player is looking backward for the first time.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
GURL: "Year one data is complete. Three seasons logged. I will walk through the record because you should see what the numbers say when you are not in the middle of making them."

Onion Man: "The offseason is for planning. The Phillies know this. Sometimes."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player views their worst cell from any season.

GURL: "That cell scored below threshold in two of three seasons. It is not the cell's fault."

Onion Man: "Listen, everybody has a bad spot. The point is you know where it is now."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION (Year 1 average above threshold)
GURL: "Year one average: above threshold on all three seasons. Clean record to build on."

Onion Man: "We did that. Three seasons. Real food potential. I need a minute."

**Unlock:** Season Journal. A persistent log recording each season's best row, worst cell, harvest highlights, and one narrator quote. Visible from the main menu.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION (Year 1 average below threshold)
GURL: "Year one data logged. There is room for improvement. Significant room."

Onion Man: "The offseason is for planning. The Phillies know this. Sometimes."

**What changes:** The Season Journal still unlocks, but GURL adds a "Year 1 Advisory" notation. It is visible every time the player opens the journal. Not punitive. Persistent.

### TRANSITION
The tarp stays on. The screen holds on the covered bed for a beat longer than comfortable. Then: "Year 2." The light shifts. Spring again. But the bed is not new anymore. The player has a record now.

## Chapter 5: "Quick Harvest"
**Year 2 — Spring | Score Target: 6.5 | Mechanic: Crop Factions and Diversity Bonus**

### OPENING
Year 2 spring. The tarp comes off faster this time. The player knows the bed. The soil looks different, darker, more worked. GURL is already talking before the grid loads.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
GURL: "You have been planting by instinct. Now plant by faction. Climbers, Fast Cycles, Brassicas, Roots, Greens, Herbs, Fruiting, Companions. Four or more factions in one season triggers a diversity bonus. Three or fewer is a monoculture, and I will treat it like one."

Onion Man: "Variety matters. Think about what you actually want to eat in June. A bed full of one thing is a field. This is not a field. This is a kitchen garden."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player places crops from a 4th faction.

GURL: "Four factions active. Diversity bonus is now in play. Continue."

Onion Man: "That is a salad, a side dish, and something to throw on the grill. Getting closer to a real meal."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Five factions represented. Diversity bonus confirmed. The bed is thinking in systems now."

Onion Man: "That is a salad, a side dish, and a garnish in one bed. Real kitchen energy."

**Unlock:** Faction Filter. The crop panel can now be filtered by faction for faster planning.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "Three factions in thirty-two cells. That is not a garden. That is a monoculture with excuses."

Onion Man: "Variety matters. In food, in life, in a rotation. We have discussed this."

**What changes:** No faction filter unlock until the player achieves 4+ factions in a future season. The system remains available but the shortcut does not.

### TRANSITION
The bed is producing real variety now. The post-season screen shows a harvest list for the first time, though it is not yet the focus. GURL: "Summer will test what spring built. Weather is not optional." The palette warms. Heat enters the frame.

## Chapter 6: "Heat Check"
**Year 2 — Summer | Score Target: 6.0 | Mechanic: Seasonal Events**

### OPENING
A weather bar appears at the top of the planning screen for the first time. It reads: "Summer forecast: sustained heat. Plan accordingly." The yard looks bright and overexposed. The concrete path is radiating. Even the cedar looks dry.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
GURL: "Seasonal events are now active. This summer includes a sustained heat forecast. Cool-season crops will take a scoring penalty during the heatwave beat. Heat-tolerant crops hold or gain. The engine predicted this. Your job is to listen."

Garden Critters (first narrative appearance): "Hot week. Weak stems. We are paying attention."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Heatwave event fires mid-season; player has 3+ cool-season crops on the board.

Critters: "Found something. Lettuce is stressed. Spinach is worse."

GURL: "Cool-season penalty applied to four cells. The forecast was available during planning."

Onion Man: "It is August. August is not kind. You have to plan for August like the Phillies plan for September: assume it will hurt and build the roster anyway."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Heat event absorbed. Warm-season crops carried the bed. Adequate preparation."

Onion Man: "We survived August. That is not nothing. The Phillies cannot always say the same."

**Unlock:** Event Forecast panel. Future seasons show a weather preview during the planning phase.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "The engine predicted this. The weather confirmed it. Your lettuce did not survive the confirmation."

Critters: "You left stress in plain sight. We just followed up."

**What changes:** Heat damage carries a minor soil penalty into the fall chapter. Critters reference the weakness in their Chapter 7 opening.

### TRANSITION
The heat breaks. The light softens. The yard exhales. Onion Man: "Fall. My season. The part where things go underground and get serious." The UI cools to deep greens and browns.

## Chapter 7: "Underground"
**Year 2 — Fall | Score Target: 5.5 | Mechanic: Row-Based Objectives**

### OPENING
The front row is highlighted. A new UI element appears: "Row Objective: Front row must be 100% Roots faction." The bed feels different when one row has a mandate. Onion Man is already looking at the front row like it owes him something.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
Onion Man: "The front row. All roots. Carrots, beets, radish, turnips. The things that do their work underground where nobody sees. I relate to this more than I should."

GURL: "Row objective is active. Row three requires complete Roots faction occupancy. Eight cells. No exceptions. The rest of the bed is yours, but that row belongs to the objective."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player fills 6 of 8 front row cells with Roots crops.

Onion Man: "Six of eight. Two more and the front row is solid. Underground union is almost at full membership."

GURL: "Six of eight compliant. Two cells remain. Do not lose focus at the threshold."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Row objective complete. Roots faction occupancy confirmed. Clean compliance."

Onion Man: "All roots. Front row. Underground union is pleased. I may have cried a little. It is biologically ambiguous."

**Unlock:** Row Objective system. Future chapters and challenges can set per-row composition targets.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "Row objective failed. Three of eight cells are non-compliant."

Onion Man: "The front row needed roots and it got chaos. I am not angry. I am disappointed. Those are different and both are happening."

**What changes:** The row objective system still unlocks, but the failed objective is logged in the Season Journal as incomplete. It becomes available as a retry challenge in Year 3.

### TRANSITION
The harvest is quiet and heavy. Root vegetables do not announce themselves. Onion Man: "Two years almost done. The bed is getting a history now." The screen dims toward winter. The second dormant season approaches.

## Chapter 8: "The Seedling"
**Year 2 — Winter | No Score Target | Mechanic: Narrative Memory**

### OPENING
The bed is covered. Winter again. But this time the review screen has a new element: a small annotation next to the tomato crop icon. It reads: "Original seedling line." The player did not put that there. The game did.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
Onion Man: "You know where the first tomato came from? The guy two doors down gave Mom a seedling. I do not remember his name. That does not matter. What matters is that the tomatoes in this bed started with a gift, and you have been planting them for two years without knowing that."

GURL: "Cedar condition: stable. Trellis wire: intact. The infrastructure is sound. I am noting the lineage tag for the record. Certain crops now carry historical markers."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player views the tomato entry in Crop Notes.

Onion Man: "Two years of scores. Two years of seasons. The bed is not just surviving. It is becoming something."

GURL: "Year two data archived. Performance trajectory: upward. That is not a compliment. That is a measurement."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION (Year 2 review)
GURL: "Year two data archived. Cedar condition: stable. Trellis wire: intact. The infrastructure is sound."

Onion Man: "Two years. The bed has seen more of you now than it saw of anyone in the years it sat empty. That counts for something even if nobody is counting."

**Unlock:** Crop Lineage tag. Certain crops carry a "lineage" marker connecting them to the bed's history. Tomatoes get the first one.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
Not applicable. No scoring this chapter.

### TRANSITION
The screen holds on the covered bed. Longer this time. Then: "Year 3." But the text feels heavier. GURL: "Year three. The last year of the campaign. Everything you have learned is expected now." The tarp comes off for the last first time.

## Chapter 9: "The Board"
**Year 3 — Spring | Score Target: 7.0 | Mechanic: Challenge Objectives**

### OPENING
Year 3 spring. The bed looks the same but the player sees it differently. Every row has memory. Every cell has history. A new panel appears: "Challenge Board." Three optional constraints are listed. The tone has shifted. The game is not teaching anymore.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
GURL: "Challenges are now available. Optional constraints you accept before planting. Trellis Compliance Audit. Diplomatic Immunity Run. Root Faction Loyalty Test. Completing a challenge adds bonus score. Failing one costs nothing except the bonus and my respect."

Onion Man: "She said optional. She did not mean optional. Year three is about proving you belong out here."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player is halfway through placement with a challenge active and currently on track.

GURL: "Challenge constraint holding at midpoint. Do not congratulate yourself yet. The back half is where discipline breaks."

Onion Man: "That was not luck. That is planning with teeth. Keep going."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Challenge passed. Constraint honored. Score above threshold. The bed operated under pressure and held."

Onion Man: "That was not luck. That was planning with teeth."

**Unlock:** Challenge Ladder. A persistent progression of challenge tiers with medals.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "Challenge failed. The constraint was clear. The execution was not. Retry with intent, not hope."

Onion Man: "Close. That was close. I could feel it almost working."

**What changes:** The challenge remains available for retry in Chapter 10. The failed attempt is logged. GURL references it if the player retries.

### TRANSITION
Spring ends with proof of competence. The bed held under pressure. GURL: "Summer requires everything. All cells. All systems. No buffer." The grid pulses once. Every cell is about to matter.

## Chapter 10: "Full House"
**Year 3 — Summer | Score Target: 7.0 | Mechanic: Full-Bed Scoring (32/32 cells)**

### OPENING
The grid loads with a counter in the corner: "0/32." Every cell is empty and every cell must be filled. The yard is at peak summer. The light is aggressive. Vegeman is back. He is leaning against the fence with his arms crossed, grinning.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
Vegeman: "Remember me? Chapter 2? I put a tomato on the ground and it fell over? I have been watching you for eight chapters. You are about to do the thing I always wanted to do. Fill. Every. Cell."

GURL: "All thirty-two cells must be occupied and score above threshold. No buffer cells. No empty padding. Every placement decision ripples across the entire board. This is the skill check."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player fills cell 28 of 32. Four remaining.

Vegeman: "Four cells left. Four. You are so close to the thing I tried to do drunk on ambition in Chapter 2. Except you are doing it right. I am confused and impressed."

GURL: "Twenty-eight cells placed. Current average is holding. Four remaining cells will determine whether this bed is full or just crowded."

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "All thirty-two cells scored. Average above threshold. No hard violations. This bed is operating at capacity."

Vegeman: "You did the thing I always wanted to do, but you did it smart. Honestly, I am a little jealous."

**Unlock:** Bed Density badge. Persistent marker of full-bed mastery. Unlocks "dense planting" challenge variant.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
GURL: "Thirty-two cells, eleven violations. Filling the bed is not the same as planning the bed."

Vegeman: "Okay, that one might have been too full. Is that a thing? I did not think that was a thing."

**What changes:** The player enters Chapter 11 without the Density badge. GURL notes the full-bed attempt in the transition. The bed's capacity remains proven even in failure; the player knows they can fill it.

### TRANSITION
Vegeman leaves again. No goodbye. He just stops being there. The summer fades. Fall light arrives and with it, something the player has not seen before: the post-season screen does not lead with the score. It leads with a list. GURL: "Fall. Harvest inventory active. What grows now is what you take inside."

## Chapter 11: "Sauce"
**Year 3 — Fall | Score Target: Any passing | Mechanic: Harvest Inventory**

### OPENING
The planning screen has a new element in the corner. Not a score target. A harvest checklist, grayed out: Tomatoes. Basil. Peppers. Onions. Carrots. The player has not been told what this list means. It is just there. The fall light is golden and heavy. The bed is waiting for its last planted season.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
GURL: "Harvest inventory is now active. Post-simulation, the bed reports what it produced, not just how it scored. The ingredient list is a new output. Plan for yield, not just points."

Onion Man: "Fall is when the garden pays you back. Or does not. Depends on what you put in and what you planned for. Think about what you actually want to carry inside when it gets cold."

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player places a crop that checks off the 3rd ingredient on the harvest list.

Onion Man is quiet for a beat. Then:

Onion Man: "Three of five. You are building toward something. I am not going to say what. But you are building toward something."

### SAUCE SEQUENCE (Full Beat-by-Beat)

**Beat 1 — Planning Phase:**
The harvest checklist sits in the corner. Five ingredients, grayed out. The player plans. No character speaks about the list directly. GURL gives her standard placement advisories. Onion Man comments on individual crops as they go in, but does not reference the list.

**Beat 2 — Season Commit:**
The player locks the season. Placements are final. The simulation runs. The standard scoring sequence fires. GURL delivers the score verdict first, as always.

GURL: "Season score calculated. Average: [score]. Passing threshold met."

**Beat 3 — The Shift:**
The score recedes. The screen does not transition to the normal post-season summary. Instead, the harvest inventory panel expands. One by one, the ingredients appear as the bed reports what it produced.

Tomatoes. The first line item lights up.

Basil. Second.

Peppers. Third.

Onions. Fourth.

Carrots. Fifth.

Each one appears with a half-second pause between them. No fanfare. No animation beyond the text appearing.

**Beat 4 — GURL's Line:**
GURL: "Harvest inventory matches target recipe. Yield sufficient. Season objective complete."

She says it like a compliance report. She does not explain what the recipe is. She does not name it.

**Beat 5 — The Silence:**
A beat of nothing. The list sits on screen. Five ingredients. All lit. The yard ambient sound continues. A screen door somewhere. The distant hum of Broad Street.

**Beat 6 — Onion Man:**
He does not speak immediately. When he does, he reads the list. Slowly. Not dramatically. Like he is checking it against something he remembers.

Onion Man: "Tomatoes. Basil. Peppers. Onions. Carrots."

A pause.

Onion Man: "That is the list. That is her list."

Another pause. Shorter.

Onion Man: "She would have made sauce with this. I am not crying. The onion is crying."

**Beat 7 — The Unlock:**
Mom's Recipe card appears in the Season Journal. It does not contain the recipe. It contains the harvest list and the date. That is enough.

No other character speaks. Vegeman does not appear. The Critters are silent. The screen holds on the harvest list and the Recipe card for as long as the player lets it.

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
GURL: "Harvest inventory matches target recipe. Yield sufficient. Season objective complete."

Onion Man: "Tomatoes. Basil. Peppers. Onions. Carrots. That is the list. That is her list. She would have made sauce with this. I am not crying. The onion is crying."

**Unlock:** Mom's Recipe card. Persistent item in the Season Journal. Contains the harvest list and the date.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
Onion Man: "We are close. We are missing [ingredient]. The recipe needs all of them and the bed gave us most of them. Most is not enough for sauce. Most is enough for next year."

GURL: "Harvest inventory incomplete for target recipe. Recommend adjusting fall composition."

**What changes:** The Recipe card does not unlock. The incomplete harvest list is saved in the Season Journal with the missing ingredients noted. Chapter 12 acknowledges the near-miss. Onion Man's final line in Chapter 12 shifts to reference the attempt rather than the completion. The game does not offer a retry. The season is over. The bed gave what it gave.

### TRANSITION
The harvest list stays on screen as the light fades. Fall ends. The last winter begins. No GURL transition line this time. Just the light dimming and the bed going quiet. The tarp appears. The final cover.

## Chapter 12: "Endures"
**Year 3 — Winter | No Score Target | Mechanic: Legacy View**

### OPENING
The bed is covered. The yard is gray and still. The UI shifts. The grid disappears. In its place: the Legacy View. Twelve season cards arranged in a row. Three years. Four seasons each. Every score. Every best row. Every worst cell. Every challenge. Every harvest. Every narrator quote saved in the Season Journal. The complete record of what the player built in 32 squares of cedar and soil.

### MECHANIC INTRODUCTION
There is no mechanic to teach. The Legacy View teaches itself. The player scrolls through their own history. The game is quiet while they do.

### MID-CHAPTER BEAT
**Trigger:** Player views the Chapter 11 harvest card in the Legacy View.

If sauce was completed:
Onion Man: "There it is. The fall we made sauce. That card is not going anywhere."

If sauce was not completed:
Onion Man: "There it is. The fall we almost made sauce. That card is not going anywhere either."

### EPILOGUE

**GURL's Final Assessment (3 sentences, her longest speech):**

GURL: "Twelve seasons logged. The bed is structurally sound and the operator demonstrated consistent improvement under escalating constraint. I do not give compliments, but I am noting for the record that this bed was maintained with intention, and that is not common."

**Onion Man's Final Line (1 sentence, his shortest):**

If sauce was completed:
Onion Man: "The bed is ready for next year, and so are you."

If sauce was not completed:
Onion Man: "The bed is ready for next year."

**The Difference:**
Two words. "And so are you." Present if the sauce was made. Absent if it was not. The player will notice. The game does not explain.

### SUCCESS RESOLUTION
Not applicable. This chapter is not graded. The Legacy View is the resolution.

**Unlock:** Free play opens. All mechanics, challenges, events, factions, and the Season Journal carry forward. The campaign is over. The bed continues.

### FAILURE RESOLUTION
Not applicable.

### TRANSITION
The final screen: the bed, covered, in the gray yard. The legacy record below it. No prompt. No "play again" button for a few seconds. Just the bed, waiting for spring. Then the free play option appears. The player knows what to do when spring comes, because they learned.

## DIALOGUE SEQUENCING RULES

1. After simulation resolves, GURL speaks first. Always.
2. Onion Man speaks second. Always.
3. Vegeman speaks only in Chapters 2, 10, and specific chaos/tutorial triggers.
4. Garden Critters speak only during events and environmental pressure beats.
5. No character breaks the fourth wall.
6. No em dashes in dialogue.
7. No ellipses in dialogue.
8. The word "journey" is never used.
9. Phillies references follow seasonal cadence: spring (hope and opener energy), summer (grind and pennant race), fall (tension and October pressure), winter (hot stove speculation and offseason trades).
10. Mom is never shown, never quoted, never given dialogue. She exists in the bed, the trellis, the cedar, and what other characters say about her.
11. Every line fires from a deterministic game-state trigger. No random dialogue selection.

## EMOTIONAL ARCHITECTURE NOTES

**What the player feels and when:**

- Chapters 1-2: Curiosity and first friction. The bed is a puzzle. The characters are entertaining but sharp.
- Chapters 3-4: Growing familiarity. The bed is becoming the player's. The winter review makes them accountable to their own record.
- Chapters 5-6: Confidence and first real pressure. The system is deeper than they thought. The environment pushes back.
- Chapters 7-8: Quiet competence and inherited weight. The roots chapter and the seedling story land because the player has been doing the work. They did not earn sentiment. They earned context.
- Chapters 9-10: Mastery under constraint. The game respects the player enough to demand more. Vegeman's return recontextualizes the entire arc.
- Chapter 11: The harvest list. No speech. No crescendo. Just five ingredients and one man reading them aloud.
- Chapter 12: Two words present or absent. The bed, covered. The record, complete. The game, continuing.

**What the game never does:**

- Tells the player they should feel moved.
- Plays music designed to trigger tears.
- Has a character deliver a monologue about loss or memory.
- Uses the word "healing" in any context.
- Shows Mom.
- Explains why the sauce matters. The player either understands or they do not. The game trusts them.
