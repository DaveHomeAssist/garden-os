Build Profile

Vertical rear cedar trellis wall, 24" hardware cloth barrier on all four sides, wire-enclosed front section, double front doors on a center post. No cattle panel tunnel arch — simpler, lighter, easier to build solo, and true to the actual structure.

Assumption: Two 4×4 raised beds already exist side by side, giving a combined footprint near 96" × 48". The enclosure sits flush with the outer bed edges. The rear trellis is anchored to the back posts screwed directly into the bed frame.

Trellis option: A premade cedar lattice panel (4×8 ft) is the fastest route. Stain before installing. Screwed to the rear frame rails, it takes about 2 hours total including the frame. Scratch-built gives more control over spacing.
Mesh strategy: Hardware cloth only where it matters — lower 24" on all sides and lower half of doors. Chicken wire handles the upper side zones above 24". Do not overskin unless animals prove they deserve escalation.

Premade vs. Scratch-Built

Premade cedar lattice: stain, cut to width if needed, screw to the rear frame. Scratch-built from 1×3 cedar gives more control over spacing. Either way, stain before you install. Cedar weathers fast without a finish.

Planting by Zone

What goes where, and why — three zones, three strategies
Planting
Zone logic: Climbers at the back against the trellis. Protected herbs and greens in the wire-enclosed middle. Low easy-harvest crops at the front near the doors.
🪴 Zone 1 — Rear Trellis Row

Vining and tall crops planted directly at the base of the rear lattice. Plants find the verticals on their own — tie loosely only if they need early guidance.

🪴 Trellis zone
🍅 Cherry Tomato

Sun Gold or Sweet Million. Tie to trellis verticals. Heavy producer by July. Use silicone clips, not wire.

🪴 Trellis zone
🥒 Beit Alpha Cucumber

Vines wind up the lattice naturally. Harvest every 2–3 days at peak season or they go bitter fast.

🪴 Trellis zone
🫘 Pole Beans

Fastest climbers on the trellis. Kentucky Wonder is reliable. No tying needed after the first week.

🪴 Trellis zone
🫛 Snap Peas

Spring only. Use the trellis for peas first, then succession with beans when heat arrives.

🛡 Zone 2 — Protected Wire Enclosure

Wire sides keep rabbits and squirrels out of these rows. Perfect for herbs and tender greens.

🛡 Protected zone
🌿 Basil

Mom's Basil. Genovese for pesto. Compact Globe for small squares. Thrives in the cage microclimate.

🛡 Protected zone
🥬 Leaf Lettuce

Salad Bowl or Red Sails. Harvest outer leaves and it regrows for weeks.

🛡 Protected zone
🥬 Bok Choy

Baby Bok Choy likes the partial shade from trellis crops. Succession every 3 weeks.

🛡 Protected zone
🌿 Cilantro

Slow bolt variety in the shadier corner. Succession every 2–3 weeks.

✋ Zone 3 — Front Access Row

Closest to the doors. Low-maintenance crops you harvest frequently or want visible from the path.

✋ Front access
🌰 Radish

24 day Cherry Belle. Fill gaps between other plants and re-sow continuously.

✋ Front access
🌼 Marigold

French Marigold at the bed edges. Best pest deterrent and a visual anchor for the front row.

✋ Front access
🌸 Nasturtium

Jewel Mix. Edible flowers, trap crop for aphids, beautiful all season with almost no effort.

✋ Front access
🫘 Bush Beans

Provider variety handles partial shade. Compact habit. Great succession crop with radish.
